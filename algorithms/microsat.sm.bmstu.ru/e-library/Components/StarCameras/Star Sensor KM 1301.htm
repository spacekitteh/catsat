Description

The Star Sensor KM 1301 provides inertial and relative attitude determination for small satellites. The optical head with a CCD detector and the processing electronics is integrated into a single small light-weight box. The sensor is fully autonomous and can deliver various data formats to the spacecraft computer. The KM 1301 is available as a low-cost, off-the-shelf product since 1995. The predecessor of the sensor was developed by the Technical University of Berlin and was successfully flown on the TUBSAT-A and TUBSAT-B satellites.

Features

  • Star Sensor body: 112 x 115 x 115 mm­
  • Height: 105 mm (w/o baffle), 170 mm (w/baffle)
  • Mass: 0.78 kg
  • Power: typical 4.2 W @ 12 V DC
  • Field of view: 21 x 31°
  • Update frequency: 4 Hz
  • Accuracy: +/- 0.02° (2 sigma)
  • Time to first acquisition: 0.5 s
  • Data interface: RS422/485
  • Operating modes: inertial, relative, camera

Application Fields

Attitude determination for satellite or experiment platforms.

State of the Art

The sensor provides a complete 3-axis attitude solution in a very compact unit and at a competitive price.

Availability

The KM 1301 is available as an off-the-shelf product.

R&D Investments

Redesign and qualification of the sensor was done in 1995 with Kayser-Threde R&D funds.
